About

Our inspiration was a mutual feeling of not being as informed about the world as we thought we should be. We wondered what stopped us from sitting down and reading the news like our parents did. In an ever-changing and fast-paced world, we realized that our attention spans were simply not as good sas older generations. Therefore, we set out to build an app to help compile the most important news for each day in short-form content like the TikTok and Instagram reels that are extremely popular nowadays.

Effectively, we combine TikTok and modern news channels to create short-form news articles that can be read in under 2 minutes. These news articles are up to date from the day surrounding current world news and politics. Our aim was to help connect the world better by keeping everyone (even people with short attention spans!) informed about what's happening in our world.

Each day, we scrape thousands of news channels using the Perigon API and summarize them using OpenAI. Then we push them to our web server after storing them in a MySQL database. We plan on making this an IOS app after HackSC, but since our members knew better how to make Web Applications, we demoed our product as a web app.

Actress Lori Loughlin has agreed to pay $500,000 in college tuition for two students as part of her sentence for her involvement in the college admissions scandal. This payment is aimed at compensating the education costs for students who were affected by the scandal and is part of Loughlin's effort to rectify the harm caused by her actions. In 2020, Loughlin and her husband, fashion designer Mossimo Giannulli, were among the many wealthy parents charged in the scheme for bribing college officials and committing fraud in order to secure their children's admissions into top universities. Both Loughlin and Giannulli pleaded guilty to the charges and were sentenced to prison time, community service, and fines. The college admissions scandal drew widespread attention and sparked a national conversation about the unequal opportunities and privileges in the college admission process. Loughlin's payment of tuition for the affected students is a step towards restoring justice and fairness in the system.
